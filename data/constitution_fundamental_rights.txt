=== Article 4: Right of Individuals to be Dealt with in Accordance with Law ===
This Article establishes a foundational constitutional guarantee that every citizen, wherever he may be, and every other person within Pakistan, shall enjoy the protection of law and be treated strictly in accordance with law. It prohibits arbitrary actions by the State by declaring that no action detrimental to the life, liberty, body, reputation, or property of any person shall be taken except in accordance with law. It further implies that executive discretion must be exercised within legal limits and subject to judicial review. The Article embodies the rule of law, ensuring that all actions of public authorities must have lawful authority and cannot be based on personal will, caprice, or abuse of power.

=== Article 8: Laws Inconsistent with Fundamental Rights to be Void ===
This Article gives supremacy to Fundamental Rights by declaring that any law, or any custom or usage having the force of law, which is inconsistent with the rights conferred by this Chapter, shall be void to the extent of such inconsistency. It also restricts the State from making laws that take away or abridge these rights. Courts are empowered to strike down unconstitutional laws. However, certain laws may be protected through constitutional provisions or amendments. This Article ensures that Fundamental Rights are enforceable and not merely declaratory, forming the basis of constitutional review and protection against legislative or executive overreach.

=== Article 9: Security of Person ===
Article 9 guarantees that no person shall be deprived of life or liberty except in accordance with law. The term “life” has been interpreted broadly by courts to include quality of life, dignity, and basic human rights. “Liberty” includes freedom from unlawful detention and restraint. The Article prohibits arbitrary deprivation of life or liberty and requires lawful justification for any such action. It forms the basis for protection against extrajudicial killings, unlawful detention, and abuse of authority. Judicial interpretation has expanded its scope to cover environmental rights, fair living conditions, and other essential aspects of human existence.

=== Article 10: Safeguards as to Arrest and Detention ===
This Article provides procedural safeguards to persons arrested or detained. It requires that a person must be informed, as soon as possible, of the grounds of arrest and must not be denied the right to consult and be defended by a legal practitioner of his choice. It also mandates that the arrested person be produced before a magistrate within twenty-four hours, excluding travel time, and prohibits detention beyond that period without judicial authority. In cases of preventive detention, additional safeguards and limitations apply. The Article ensures protection against arbitrary arrest and guarantees minimum procedural fairness in criminal justice.

=== Article 10-A: Right to Fair Trial ===
This Article guarantees that for determination of civil rights and obligations or in any criminal charge, a person is entitled to a fair trial and due process. It constitutionalizes principles of natural justice, including impartial tribunal, right to be heard, transparency, and reasoned decisions. It applies to all judicial and quasi-judicial proceedings. Courts have interpreted it broadly to include access to justice, legal representation, and procedural fairness. Any law or action violating fair trial standards may be challenged as unconstitutional. It strengthens rule of law by ensuring justice is not only done but seen to be done.

=== Article 14: Inviolability of Dignity of Man ===
Article 14 declares that the dignity of man and, subject to law, the privacy of home shall be inviolable. It explicitly prohibits torture for extracting evidence. The concept of dignity includes respect for physical and mental integrity, honor, and personal autonomy. Privacy of home protects individuals from unlawful intrusion by the State or others. Courts have expanded this right to include protection against degrading treatment and unlawful surveillance. The Article acts as a safeguard against abuse of power and ensures humane treatment of individuals in all circumstances, including during investigation and detention.

=== Article 15: Freedom of Movement ===
This Article grants every citizen the right to remain in, enter, move freely throughout Pakistan, and reside or settle in any part thereof. However, this right is subject to reasonable restrictions imposed by law in the public interest, such as security, public health, or order. Restrictions must not be arbitrary and should be proportionate. The Article ensures mobility and unity within the State, enabling citizens to live and work anywhere in the country. It also protects against unlawful exile or restriction of movement without legal authority.

=== Article 16: Freedom of Assembly ===
Article 16 guarantees the right of citizens to assemble peacefully and without arms. This right is essential for democratic participation, protest, and expression of collective views. However, it is subject to reasonable restrictions imposed by law in the interest of public order. Authorities may regulate assemblies to prevent violence, disorder, or threats to security, but cannot arbitrarily prohibit peaceful gatherings. The Article balances individual freedom with societal stability, ensuring that public demonstrations remain lawful and orderly.

=== Article 17: Freedom of Association ===
This Article provides that every citizen has the right to form associations or unions, including political parties. It is subject to reasonable restrictions in the interest of sovereignty or integrity of Pakistan, public order, or morality. The Article also ensures that political parties operate within legal frameworks. It promotes collective organization for social, economic, and political purposes. Courts have emphasized that restrictions must be justified and not excessive. This right is fundamental to democratic governance and civil society development.

=== Article 18: Freedom of Trade, Business or Profession ===
Article 18 guarantees every citizen the right to enter upon any lawful profession or occupation and to conduct any lawful trade or business. This right is subject to qualifications prescribed by law, including licensing, regulation, and professional standards. The State may regulate economic activities in the public interest, but cannot impose arbitrary restrictions. The Article ensures economic freedom while allowing reasonable control to maintain standards, protect consumers, and ensure fair competition.

=== Article 19: Freedom of Speech and Expression ===
This Article guarantees freedom of speech and expression, as well as freedom of the press. However, it is subject to reasonable restrictions imposed by law in the interest of the glory of Islam, integrity, security, or defence of Pakistan, public order, decency, or morality. The right includes expression through words, writing, or media. Courts have emphasized that restrictions must be narrowly tailored. This Article forms the backbone of democratic discourse, enabling criticism, debate, and dissemination of ideas while balancing societal interests.

=== Article 19-A: Right to Information ===
Article 19-A grants citizens the right to access information in matters of public importance. It promotes transparency and accountability in governance. The right is subject to regulation and reasonable restrictions, such as national security or privacy concerns. It enables citizens to scrutinize public institutions and participate effectively in democratic processes. Laws like right to information statutes operationalize this Article. Courts have recognized it as essential for informed decision-making and prevention of corruption.

=== Article 20: Freedom to Profess Religion ===
This Article guarantees every citizen the right to profess, practice, and propagate his religion, subject to law, public order, and morality. It also grants religious denominations the right to establish, maintain, and manage their institutions. The State must ensure religious tolerance and non-discrimination. However, practices that disturb public order or violate law may be regulated. The Article protects both individual and collective religious freedom within a constitutional framework.

=== Article 23: Provision as to Property ===
Article 23 provides that every citizen has the right to acquire, hold, and dispose of property in any part of Pakistan, subject to the Constitution and reasonable restrictions imposed by law in the public interest. The State may regulate property ownership for purposes such as planning, taxation, or land reforms. However, such regulation must be lawful and not arbitrary. The Article recognizes property as an important economic right while allowing necessary limitations.

=== Article 24: Protection of Property Rights ===
This Article protects individuals from compulsory deprivation of property except in accordance with law. It generally requires compensation where property is acquired by the State for public purposes. The law must specify the authority, purpose, and manner of acquisition. Arbitrary confiscation is prohibited. The Article ensures fairness in state acquisition and balances public needs with private rights. Courts scrutinize such actions to prevent misuse of power.

=== Article 25: Equality of Citizens ===
Article 25 declares that all citizens are equal before law and entitled to equal protection of law. It prohibits discrimination on the basis of sex alone but allows reasonable classification and affirmative action for disadvantaged groups. The principle ensures that laws apply equally and fairly to all individuals. Courts examine whether classifications are reasonable and have a rational nexus to the objective. This Article forms the cornerstone of equality and non-discrimination in constitutional law.

=== Article 25-A: Right to Education ===
Article 25-A mandates the State to provide free and compulsory education to all children aged five to sixteen years in such manner as determined by law. It imposes a positive obligation on the State to ensure access to education. The Article recognizes education as a fundamental right essential for personal development and societal progress. Implementation depends on legislative and administrative measures, including infrastructure, funding, and policy frameworks.
